Below is an up-to-date, investor-style brief on Rivian Automotive (NASDAQ: RIVN) as of December 17, 2025. Spot checks use company filings and reputable outlets; I note dates explicitly where relevant.

1) Company overview - Business and model - U.S. EV OEM focused on “adventure” consumer vehicles (R1T pickup, R1S SUV) and commercial vans (EDV). Direct-to-consumer sales, in‑house software, service network, and charging (Rivian Adventure Network). New driver-assistance subscriptions (Autonomy+) and connected services add recurring revenue opportunities. (businesswire.com) - Upcoming mid-size platform: R2 (starting around $45,000, first-half 2026), followed by R3/R3X at lower price points; initial R2 production shifted to Normal, Illinois to save >$2B and accelerate timing. (investing.com) - Strategic JV with Volkswagen (RV Tech) to co-develop software-defined vehicle (SDV) architecture; VW investment expected up to ~$5.8B across tranches and the JV now has >1,500 engineers. (volkswagen-group.com) - Competitive position - Positioned as a premium EV “adventure” brand; competition includes Tesla (Model Y/X), legacy OEMs (GM, Ford), and other EV startups (Lucid). Rivian is deepening vertical integration (own autonomy chip, zonal/SDV stack with VW) to differentiate on software and cost. (reuters.com) - Commercial vans: Amazon remains key, but exclusivity ended; first non‑Amazon fleet customer HelloFresh adopted 70 vans in 2025. (techcrunch.com)
